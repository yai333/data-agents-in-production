Chinook Digital Media Store - Institutional Knowledge
=====================================================

Fiscal Calendar and Reporting Periods
-------------------------------------
The company operates on a calendar fiscal year (January 1 through December 31).
Quarterly reporting follows the standard calendar quarters: Q1 = Jan-Mar,
Q2 = Apr-Jun, Q3 = Jul-Sep, Q4 = Oct-Dec. When stakeholders say "last quarter"
they mean the most recently completed calendar quarter, not a rolling 90-day
window. Monthly reports are due by the 5th business day of the following month.

Revenue Recognition and Pricing
--------------------------------
Revenue is recognized at the point of sale (invoice creation), not at payment
collection. All prices are in USD regardless of customer location. There are two
price tiers: standard tracks at $0.99 and premium/long-form content at $1.99.
The invoice total field is the source of truth for revenue — do not recalculate
from unit_price * quantity in invoice_line unless investigating discrepancies.

Customer Definitions and Segmentation
--------------------------------------
A customer is classified as "active" if they have at least one invoice within
the last 90 days. A "churned" customer has no invoice in the last 90 days.
"High-value" customers are those in the top 10% by lifetime spending. The
support_rep_id field links customers to their assigned sales support agent;
customers without a support rep (NULL) are self-service web customers.

Data Quality Notes
------------------
Invoice dates range from 2009 to 2013 in the original dataset. Some employee
records use Canadian postal code format (e.g., "T2P 5M5") while customer
postal codes vary by country. The billing_address fields on invoices are
copied from the customer record at time of purchase and may not reflect
current customer addresses. Employee birth_date and hire_date fields use
TIMESTAMP WITHOUT TIME ZONE — treat them as date-only values.

Known Quirks and Edge Cases
----------------------------
The playlist table contains both user-created playlists and system-generated
playlists (e.g., "Music", "Movies", "TV Shows"). When counting "user playlists",
exclude playlist names that match media type categories. The track.composer
field uses "/" as a delimiter for multiple composers — do not split on commas.
Some tracks have NULL composer values, which means the composer is unknown,
not that the track is an instrumental.
